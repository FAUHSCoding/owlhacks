About OwlHacks 2025

We are excited to announce OwlHacks 2025 — FAUHS’s first-ever entirely student-run hackathon. We’re giving you 36 hours to build whatever wild, quirky, or surprisingly brilliant project you can dream up. Whether you’re a code ninja or just here for the free pizza, bring your best (or worst) ideas and compete for some truly amazing (and unique) prizes. So get ready to experience what happens when passionate students gather to create, problem-solve, and make some questionable design choices along the way — OwlHacks 2025: where great ideas come to life.

Hackathon Tracks

Web Development

From sleek portfolios to the most cursed websites imaginable, the Web Development track is your playground. Whether you're building the next big thing or just an inside joke in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, this track is all about bringing your best ideas to the web. Show off your skills, embrace the chaos, and maybe even walk away with a prize.

Hardware

The Hardware track is where you bring your gadgets, microcontrollers, sensors, and all things physical to life. With Arduinos and a variety of sensors available for your hacking pleasure, you’ve got all the tools you need to make something awesome (or just ridiculously fun). Whether you're building a smart toaster or hacking together a sensor network, this track is all about creating functional—or hilariously dysfunctional—hardware projects. Dive into embedded software, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, and anything that can blink, beep, or buzz. Bring your code to the real world and impress us with your creations—or at least with how much you can get to blink before the deadline.

Game Development

Ready to turn your wildest game ideas into playable chaos? The Game Development track is where you get to build everything from simple 2D games to mind-bending experiences. Whether you’re coding your dream game or creating a hilarious mess of pixels and code, this track is all about having fun while making something that (hopefully) works. Use any engine, framework, or game design tool you want—Unity, Godot, you name it. Get ready for a challenge where creativity and gameplay take center stage, and anything can happen.

Open-Ended

The Open-Ended Track is for the hackers who don’t fit neatly into a box. Have a project idea that doesn’t align with any of the other categories? This track is where you can unleash your creativity, experiment, and bring your vision to life without any constraints. Whether you're building a new tool, crafting an unconventional solution, or just hacking together something fun, the Open-Ended Track welcomes all kinds of projects. If you can imagine it, you can build it— no theme, no limitations, just pure hacking potential.

Awards

Best Beginner Hacks

This award celebrates the freshest ideas from participants just starting their hackathon journey. It’s not about how much you know, but how well you communicate your concept and bring it to life. Creativity and clarity in presentation are key here—if you can explain your idea in a way that makes everyone go, “That’s genius!” you’ve already won half the battle.

Placements: TBD

Best Advanced Hacks

This award recognizes the most technically impressive projects that push the limits of coding, design, and execution. While complexity is a major factor, we don’t forget about the power of a strong idea. If your hack blends ingenuity with intricate execution, presenting something that’s both brilliant and functional, you’ve got a solid shot at this one.

Placements: TBD

Track Awards

Best Web Dev Project, Best Game, Best Hardware Project - these awards recognize the most technically impressive projects in each track. If your hack blends ingenuity with intricate execution, presenting something that’s both brilliant and functional, you’ve got a solid shot at this one.

Placements: Max 9 (can be adjusted)
